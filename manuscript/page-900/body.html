Chapter Xli

The Journey's End

'I see my way as birds their trackless way—
 I shall arrive! what time, what circuit first,
 I ask not: but unless God send his hail
 Or blinding fire-balls, sleet, or stifling snow,
 In some time—his good time—I shall arrive;
 He guides me and the bird. In His good time!'
        BROWNING'S PARACELSUS.

So the winter was getting on, and the days were beginning to lengthen, without bringing with them any of the brightness of hope which usually accompanies the rays of a February sun. Mrs. Thornton had of course entirely ceased to come to the house. Mr. Thornton came occasionally, but his visits were addressed to her father, and were confined to the study.
