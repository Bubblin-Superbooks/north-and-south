Chapter Xiv

The Mutiny

'I was used
 To sleep at nights as sweetly as a child,—
 Now if the wind blew rough, it made me start,
 And think of my poor boy tossing about
 Upon the roaring seas. And then I seemed
 To feel that it was hard to take him from me
 For such a little fault.'
         SOUTHEY.

It was a comfort to Margaret about this time, to find that her mother drew more tenderly and intimately towards her than she had ever done since the days of her childhood. She took her to her heart as a confidential friend—the post Margaret had always longed to fill, and had envied Dixon for being preferred to. Margaret
