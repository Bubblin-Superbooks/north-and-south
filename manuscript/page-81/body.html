Chapter Iv

Doubts And Difficulties

'Cast me upon some naked shore,
 Where I may tracke
 Only the print of some sad wracke,
 If thou be there, though the seas roare,
 I shall no gentler calm implore.'
         HABINGTON.

He was gone. The house was shut up for the evening. No more deep blue skies or crimson and amber tints. Margaret went up to dress for the early tea, finding Dixon in a pretty temper from the interruption which a visitor had naturally occasioned on a busy day.
