Chapter Xxxiv

False And True

'Truth will fail thee never, never!
 Though thy bark be tempest-driven,
 Though each plank be rent and riven,
 Truth will bear thee on for ever!'
         ANON.

The 'bearing up better than likely' was a terrible strain upon Margaret. Sometimes she thought she must give way, and cry out with pain, as the sudden sharp thought came across her, even during her apparently cheerful conversations with her father, that she had no longer a mother.
