Chapter Xxxix

Making Friends

'Nay, I have done; you get no more of me:
 And I am glad, yea glad with all my heart,
 That thus so clearly I myself am free.'
         DRAYTON.

Margaret shut herself up in her own room, after she had quitted Mrs. Thornton. She began to walk backwards and forwards, in her old habitual way of showing agitation; but, then, remembering that in that slightly-built house every step was heard from one room to another, she sate down until she heard Mrs. Thornton go safely out of the house.
